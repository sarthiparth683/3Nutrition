BMR(Basal Metabolic Rate)

  • To calculate BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate), you can use the Harris-Benedict Equation. It estimates how many calories your body needs at rest for basic functions like breathing, heartbeat, digestion, etc.

    • Harris-Benedict Formula For BMR

  • For Men - 88.362 + (13.397 x {weight in kg}) + (4.799 x {height in cm}) - (5.677 X {age in years})
  • For Women - 447.593 + (9.247 x {weight in kg}) + (3.098 x {height in cm}) - (4.330 x {age in years})

    • Activity Factor required for calorie needs

  • Sedentary (little/no exercise) | 1.2 |
  • Light exercise (1–3 days/week) | 1.375 |
  • Moderate exercise (3–5 days/week) | 1.55 |
  • Heavy exercise (6–7 days/week) | 1.725 |
  • Very heavy exercise (physical job + training) | 1.9 |

    • TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)

  • TDEE = BMR x Activity Factor
